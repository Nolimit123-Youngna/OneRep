What is Chess?

Chess is one of the world's most popular strategy games! It's played on an 8√ó8 board with 64 squares arranged in a checkered pattern. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns.

The goal is simple: capture your opponent's king by putting it in "checkmate" - a position where the king cannot escape capture!

Meet the Chess Pieces

King

The most important piece! Moves one square in any direction.

‚ôï

Queen

The most powerful piece! Moves any number of squares in any direction.

Rook

Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

‚ôó

Bishop

Moves any number of squares diagonally.

‚ôò

Knight

Moves in an "L" shape: 2 squares in one direction, then 1 square perpendicular.

‚ôô

Pawn

Moves forward one square, captures diagonally. Can move two squares on first move!

Basic Rules

  • White always moves first
  • Players take turns moving one piece at a time
  • You capture opponent pieces by moving to their square
  • You cannot move into check (where your king would be captured)
  • The game ends when one king is in checkmate, or by stalemate/draw

üåü Beginner Tips

Control the center: Try to place your pieces where they control the four central squares of the board.

Develop your pieces: Get your knights and bishops out early in the game!

Castle early: This special move helps protect your king and develops your rook.

Think before you move: Always ask "What is my opponent threatening?" before making your move.

Special Moves

Castling: A special move involving the king and a rook that helps protect your king.

En Passant: A special pawn capture that can happen when an opponent's pawn moves two squares forward.

Pawn Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it can become any piece (usually a queen)!

Ready to Play?

Now that you know the basics, you're ready to start your chess journey! Remember, chess is a game that takes minutes to learn but a lifetime to master. The more you play, the better you'll become!

Start with simple tactics, practice with friends, and most importantly - have fun! Every chess master was once a beginner.
